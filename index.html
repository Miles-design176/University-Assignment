My Choice

Why I Chose BCIT

After researching universities across British Columbia, BCIT stood out as the clear choice for Electrical Engineering — here's why.

Industry-First Education

BCIT is built differently from traditional universities. Instructors come from the field — they've worked as engineers and technicians, which means every lecture connects directly to real-world application. BCIT graduates have a reputation for being productive from day one on the job.

Flexible Pathway

BCIT's unique structure lets me start in the common first year shared between the ECET Diploma and the BEng degree. After Year 1, I can compete for a seat in the full engineering degree — or exit with a diploma. This flexibility reduces risk while keeping the ceiling high.

Direct BC Hydro Connection

BC Hydro has a long-standing hiring relationship with BCIT graduates. The CPC Technologist Trainee Program at BC Hydro specifically requires an Electrical/Electronics Engineering Technology diploma from an accredited institution — making BCIT the most direct route to that career goal.

Team-Based Learning

BCIT's project-based curriculum means working in teams throughout the program — designing, building, and testing real systems. This suits the way I work best: collaborating with others toward a shared goal, not studying in isolation.

The Institution

About BCIT

The British Columbia Institute of Technology (BCIT) is a public polytechnic institute located on a large Burnaby campus adjacent to Deer Lake. Founded in 1964, BCIT has grown into one of Canada's most respected technical institutions, with over 44,000 students enrolled annually and more than 210,000 alumni.

Unlike a traditional university, BCIT is a polytechnic — meaning its entire mission centers on applied, hands-on education. Every program is designed to put graduates directly to work. BCIT runs five campuses across Metro Vancouver, including the main Burnaby campus where Electrical Engineering is housed.

BCIT's Electrical Engineering degree is nationally accredited by the Canadian Engineering Accreditation Board (CEAB) and recognized as a baccalaureate degree by the BC Ministry of Advanced Education — ensuring the credential is respected by employers and licensing bodies across Canada.

97% of BCIT graduates find relevant employment within their field. This is one of the highest rates of any post-secondary institution in Canada, and reflects the depth of industry connections BCIT maintains.

// THE PROGRAM

The Bachelor of Engineering in Electrical Engineering is a four-year full-time program delivered in-person at BCIT Burnaby. The first year is shared with the ECET Diploma, giving students an entry point and a decision moment. The program covers power systems, electronics, control systems, communications, and engineering mathematics — with a 4-month workplace education (co-op) experience embedded before graduation.


In final year, every student completes a capstone engineering design project — often sponsored by a real industry partner. This is the hands-on, team-based experience that defines the BCIT difference.

High School Head Start

Electrical Level 1 — Secondary School Apprenticeship

Before even starting at BCIT, I am completing my Electrical Level 1 trade certification through BC's Secondary School Apprenticeship (SSA) program — for free, while still in high school.

The Secondary School Apprenticeship (SSA) program is a BC government initiative that allows high school students to begin a formal trade apprenticeship while completing their diploma. Students log real on-the-job hours with a certified employer and complete Level 1 trade school training at no cost — the program is fully funded.

For the Electrical trade, Level 1 covers the fundamentals of electrical theory, wiring methods, the BC Electrical Code, tools and safety, and basic installation work. These are real, transferable skills that directly complement the theoretical side of the BCIT Electrical Engineering degree.

Upon completing Level 1, those hours are officially registered with SkilledTradesBC and count toward a Journeyman Electrician ticket if I choose to pursue it later in my career. The credential doesn't expire and doesn't disappear — it stays on your record permanently.

Most BCIT engineering students arrive with zero trade experience. Arriving with Electrical Level 1 already completed signals to BCIT — and future employers like BC Hydro — that I understand electrical work from the ground up, not just from a textbook.

// WHY THIS MATTERS

Completely Free

The SSA program is fully government-funded. Level 1 trade training that would normally cost hundreds of dollars is covered entirely — making this one of the smartest financial decisions available to a BC high school student.

Resume Advantage

Graduating engineers who also hold a trade credential are rare. It demonstrates initiative, hands-on experience, and a deeper understanding of the work — exactly what BC Hydro looks for when hiring new engineers and CPC Technologist Trainees.

Built-In Backup Plan

If I ever want to work in the electrical field before finishing my degree — during a gap year, to save money, or for any other reason — Level 1 plus logged apprenticeship hours means I can find paid electrical work immediately.

Future Upgrade Path

Those hours count toward a Red Seal Journeyman Electrician ticket later in my career if I ever want it. Engineers with a trade ticket command more respect on job sites and can move into roles that purely academic engineers cannot access.

Mission & Principles

Values of BCIT & School of Energy

BCIT's institutional identity is built on a set of principles that distinguish it from conventional universities. These values directly informed my choice.

01

Applied Learning

Every program at BCIT is grounded in real-world application. Theory exists to enable practice — not the other way around. Students leave with hands-on skills employers can use immediately, not just academic credentials.

02

Industry Integration

BCIT maintains deep relationships with BC's largest employers including BC Hydro, Telus, and major engineering firms. Faculty are practitioners. Capstone projects are often industry-sponsored. Connections to employment begin on day one.

03

Collaboration

The School of Energy is built around the belief that great outcomes come from engineers, technologists, and tradespeople working together. Programs are deliberately structured to reflect integrated, team-based work environments.

04

Lifelong Learning

BCIT fosters a commitment to continuous growth — evidenced by its own leadership pursuing multiple advanced degrees while working. Part-time and flexible learning options allow professionals to keep upgrading throughout their careers.

05

Sustainability

The School of Energy plays a central role in BC's clean energy future. The programs are aligned with Industry 4.0, digital transformation, and smart grid systems — preparing graduates to lead the transition to sustainable power infrastructure.

06

Inclusion & Diversity

BCIT serves over 44,000 students from more than 120 countries and actively supports Indigenous professionals through dedicated development programs. The institution is committed to building an engineering workforce that reflects the diversity of BC.

Campus Life

Groups, Clubs & Extracurriculars

BCIT has nearly 40 student clubs and a range of extracurricular opportunities. For an engineering student, several are particularly valuable for networking, skill-building, and industry connection.

IEEE BCIT Student Branch

The Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers is the world's largest professional association for electrical and tech professionals. BCIT's student branch is open to Electrical and Computer Engineering students and provides direct networking with the professional engineering community, access to technical resources, and industry events.

BCIT Robotics Club

A team of engineering and robotics enthusiasts who love designing and building bots. For an electrical engineering student with an interest in systems and automation, this club offers hands-on project experience outside the classroom — directly complementary to the power and control systems curriculum.

Engineers Without Borders

EWB BCIT connects students to global engineering challenges. Members work on humanitarian and development engineering projects, building skills in cross-cultural collaboration, sustainable design, and community-centered problem solving — skills that translate directly to team leadership in engineering environments.

BCIT Engineering Students' Society (ESS)

The ESS represents engineering students across all programs, organizes the annual Western Engineering Competition, coordinates networking events with industry, and advocates for student interests within the School of Energy. Joining early builds visibility and connections before graduation.


Extracurricular Learning

Beyond the Classroom

Co-op Work Experience

A mandatory 4-month paid work term is embedded in the BEng program. Students apply to work with BC Hydro, Fortis BC, BC Transit, Telus, and other major employers. This is the most direct path to a job offer upon graduation — and it costs only $861 in registration fees while you earn a salary.

Applied Research Projects

BCIT's applied research centre (MAKE+) lets students participate in industry-funded research. For electrical engineering students this means working on real power systems, smart grid, and control challenges with faculty and industry partners — directly relevant to a BC Hydro career path.

VEX Robotics Competition

BCIT students can participate in the VEX Robotics Competition, building and programming competitive robots. For an electrical engineering student with an interest in systems and automation, this is hands-on project experience that complements the control systems curriculum directly.

Western Engineering Competition

An annual multi-day event where engineering students from across Western Canada compete in design, consulting, and problem-solving challenges. BCIT sends teams every year. Competing builds presentation skills, engineering thinking under pressure, and a network across the industry.

Recreation & Intramural Sports

BCIT Recreation Services provides access to a gym and fitness centre in SE16, fitness classes, and organized intramural sports leagues. A healthy way to decompress during a demanding engineering program and meet students from other departments.

Enactus BCIT

A student entrepreneurship organization that works on applied community impact projects. Members develop real skills in project management, leadership, and communication — all directly valuable for an engineer aiming toward team leadership and project management roles at BC Hydro.
